This Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect personal data when you use Askar. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. Data we process and purposes

  • Account & security: Where server features are used, we may process access tokens and related authentication/refresh data to maintain your session.
  • Provisioning & connectivity: For BLE Wi-Fi provisioning and scanning, we may process device identifiers, Wi-Fi SSIDs, and credentials you provide—only to complete setup and local connectivity.
  • Local network & device control: The App communicates over your LAN with Alpaca-compatible endpoints you configure, for discovery and control; traffic is mainly local.
  • App updates: We may send app id (e.g. package name), platform, and version to the operator’s server for version checks (exact fields depend on the live API).
  • Preferences & registry: Language, username, registered devices, groups, and similar data are stored to operate app features on your device.

2. Permissions

We may request Bluetooth, Wi-Fi / nearby devices, network, and location-related permissions where the operating system requires them for scanning or BLE. You can manage permissions in system settings; denying them may limit features.

3. Storage & security

Sensitive values (e.g. access tokens, Wi-Fi passwords keyed by SSID) are stored using platform secure storage. Other preferences and device registry data may use standard local storage. Protect your device with a lock screen.

4. Network & encryption

The App may permit cleartext HTTP to specific servers or devices (e.g. lab/LAN environments). Unencrypted traffic can be intercepted on untrusted networks. Use trusted networks; production deployments should prefer HTTPS where appropriate.

During provisioning, the App may run a short-lived local HTTP endpoint to receive reports from hardware.

5. Third-party SDKs & analytics

The shipped build and our published list control. The current version does not integrate typical advertising or third-party analytics SDKs (e.g. Firebase Analytics). If that changes, we will update this Policy and obtain consent where required.

6. International transfers

Processing primarily occurs on your device and networks you connect to. If servers are located outside your country or data is transferred cross-border, we will describe recipients and safeguards as required by law (e.g. GDPR mechanisms).

7. Children

The App is not directed at children. If you believe we have collected a child’s data, contact us and we will delete it where required.

8. Your rights (EEA/UK & similar laws)

Depending on jurisdiction, you may have rights to access, rectify, erase, restrict, port data, object, and withdraw consent. Contact us using the details above. You may lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. Some data exists only on your device—you can delete it by clearing app data or uninstalling.

9. California residents (summary)

If the CCPA/CPRA applies, you may have rights to know, delete, and opt out of certain “sales/sharing” (we do not sell personal data in the conventional sense; see contact for requests). We do not use sensitive personal information for inferring characteristics for unlawful discrimination.

10. Changes

We may update this Policy. Material changes will be communicated via in-app notice or publication on our site/pages. Continued use after the effective date may constitute acceptance.
